What is Cystic Acne?

Our skin consists of sebaceous glands, which produce sebum (natural oil). When excess sebum is produced, it leads to clogged pores. This creates an environment for bacteria (P. acnes) to thrive, which in turn leads to the development of cysts in the deepest layers of the skin.

What actually causes this kind of Acne?

  • Hormonal Imbalance.
  • Taking lot of Stress.
  • Improper Medications/ Taking inappropriate medicines.
  • Poor Hygiene.
  • Using wrong or harmful skin products.
  • Exposure to Sun for longer hours.
  • Eating Junk Food in excess.

Since cystic acne is the most severe kind of acne, it requires a dermatologist intervention. According to your condition, your doctor may suggest a treatment method or combination therapy. Here are some medical treatment options:

  1. Benzoyl Peroxide:

    2. Benzoyl peroxide functions by reducing the bacteria causing cystic acne and thereby reducing inflammation. It can be used in concentrations of 2.5 - 10 %. Make sure you know your skin type and then use the right concentration of benzoyl peroxide.

  2. Retinoids:

    3. A derivative of Vitamin A, Isotretinoin can be taken in the form of tablets. This is one of the most effective forms of treating cystic acne. Isotretinoin shows results in 6-8 months of usage. Although it is very effective, it can have some side effects like mood swings, inflammatory bowel syndrome, bruising, skin inflammation, urine in blood, headaches and muscle pains. Topical retinoids are also recommended for cystic acne, even though they do not carry the same power as oral retinoids. They come in the form of creams, gels and lotions. Topical retinoids can make your skin red and make it susceptible to sunburn. Using a sunscreen is a good idea when you are on topical retinoids.

  3. Triamcinolone:

    4. Triamcinolone is a corticosteroid that can be directly injected into the cyst to reduce inflammation and soothe cystic acne. This treatment is done by a certified dermatologist.

Precautions that can keep you away from this:

  • Cleanse:
    • Wash your face twice a day in the morning and night to remove all the dirt, makeup residue and excess oils from your face. Make sure to use a gentle cleanser and not scrub too much, which can aggravate cystic acne.
  • Moisturize:
    • Hydrate your skin and let your oily skin repair its natural barrier with a good moisturizer. Make sure it is non comedogenic and oil free so that it does not clog your pores further.
  • Never Sleep With Your Makeup On:
    • No matter how tired you are, make sure to remove your makeup before hitting the bed. Also stick to non-comedogenic and oil-free makeup.
  • Sunscreen:
    • Wear sunscreen before stepping out as this can protect your skin from sunburns. A lot of cystic acne medications tend to make your skin susceptible to sunburn.
  • Kick Away Stress:
    • Practice yoga, meditation or some form of exercise to keep stress at bay. Stress is linked to acne breakouts.
  • Healthy Diet:
    • Avoid foods full of carbs, sugars and fats. Increase your intake of fresh fruits and vegetables. Pro tip: Do not touch, pick or squeeze acne. By doing so, you are only pushing the infection deeper and spreading it.
